About PickWick

Pickwick is a trademark with an impressive history. Since 1753, Pickwick has been a strong international tea brand. A brand full of flavors that offer clean and natural products that can be enjoyed at any time of the day. All over the world, millions of people drink one of the different and tasty Pickwick tea blends daily.


Since 1753, Douwe Egberts has collected a lot of experience about creating different tea blends. 1753 was the year when Egbert Douwe opened his first small shop in the Netherlands. Here he sold coffee, tea and tobacco. Until April 1937, all tea was marketed under the Douwe Egberts trademark. At that time, customers were given a teaspoon on which there was a drawing of an English mail truck. This packaging proved very popular and inspired to find an English- savvy brand name for Douwe Egbert's tea.

The director of Douwe Egberts at the time had a wife who was as fond of tea as she was of Charles Dickens' books.Inspired by his short story "Pickwick Papers", she suggested the name "Pickwick". Since then, Douwe Egbert's tea has been marketed under this brand name.

Sustainability

Tea is a global drink. Worldwide, millions of people are involved in the cultivation and production of tea. We therefore believe that the tea industry bears a great ethical responsibility and through Douwe Egberts Master Blenders (DEMB) we are committed to sustainable purchase of tea.


We do this through our membership of The Ethical Tea Partnership (ETP). Membership is in line with our efforts to contribute to long-term wearability in the tea field and it is also a logical step forward towards our ambition to constantly contribute to sustainable business practices and compliance with high ethical standards.


Etp works to monitor the conditions of tea production worldwide to ensure that tea plantations comply with the relevant laws and trade union agreements of the countries of origin with regard to employment (including minimum age and wage levels), education, maternity, etc. ETP aims to work closely with tea producers and other stakeholders to promote an open dialogue to ensure adequate living and working conditions for workers in tea plantations and to seek improvements, where necessary.
